Paul Watson: Career

Paul Watson has been active in professional basketball since his high school days. He led his high school team to a state championship and was named Most Valuable Player because of his extraordinary game skills and techniques.

After graduating high school, Watson went to California State University and played for the Fresno State Bulldogs college team.

In 2014, Watson earned the title of the Mountain West Conference Freshman of the Year. It was possible because of the player’s experience and talent.

In his Junior year of College, Watson suffered from an ankle injury. The injury was severe, and Watson had to sit back for three matches in the season.

Watson led the Bulldogs to reach the NCAA Tournament for the first time since 2001 in 2014. He played exceptionally in his senior year as well.

Paul Watson participated in the College Basketball Slam Dunk competition in 2017.

NBA G League Career

Paul Watson played with the Phoenix Suns in 2017. He was ready for the NBA draft in 2017 but did not get drafted. However, the Toronto Raptors signed him later for the Las Vegas Summer League. He played a total of five games for the club.

After gaining professional experience with the Raptors, Watson signed with the BG Göttingen of the German Basketball Bundesliga in August 2017.

His career was short-lived, as the club waived him after just one game.

 The Westchester Knicks picked Watson for the 2017 NBA G League Draft in round 1 in the same year. He displayed great moves and scores in the whole season with the Knicks.

On October 5, 2018, Watson joined the New York Knicks. Unfortunately, he was released from the contract two days later and added to the Westchester Knicks training camp roster.

For the 2019–2020 season, the Raptors 905 picked Watson from Westchester Knicks. He played the whole season brilliantly, leading the club to many victories.

Entry into NBA

On January 6, 2020, the Atlanta Hawks signed Watson for a 10-day contract deal. He was released a week later from the team.

On January 15, 2020, Toronto Raptors signed Watson for a two-way contract. He had a fantastic season with the Raptors in the year.

Believing in his capabilities and athletic abilities, the Raptors signed Watson for a multi-year contract on December 19, 2020. 

On April 16, 2021, Watson recorded a career-high with 30 points against the Orlando Magic and led his team to victory. He played for a year and a half for the Raptors and was released on  August 3, 2021. 

On September 15, 2021, Watson joined the Oklahoma City Thunder on a two-way contract with the Oklahoma City Blue, an affiliate of the Thunder.
