Shelton Benjamin | Education and Amateur Wrestling

Benjamin was raised in his hometown, Orangeburg, South Carolina, and thus attended high school at Orangeburg-Wilkinson High School.

To illustrate, he commenced his wrestling in his sophomore year with a 122–10 overall win-loss record and was a two-time South Carolina state high school heavyweight wrestling champion (1993-1994).

As for his college, he attended Lassen Community College in Susanville, California, where he claimed the championship of the National Junior College Athletic Association (NJCAA) track and field 100m.

Not to mention, he also became NJCAA collegiate wrestling champion. In 1995, Benjamin got a full football scholarship at North Carolina State University, which he attended briefly.

He transferred to the University of Minnesota on a wrestling scholarship for his further studies during his junior and senior college years.

During his tenure at the University, he recorded a 36–6 overall win-loss score in College Wrestling.

Right after his graduation, Benjamin started to work as an assistant wrestling coach at the same University.

In the meantime, he got a chance to train with future Ohio Valley Wrestling (OVW) tag team partner Brock Lesnar.

After that, he chose to pursue his career as a professional wrestler rather than qualifying for the 2000 Summer Olympics.
