Where to buy liquidation return pallets in the UK

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Saturday, May 25, 2024

MAKING money from old and returned stock is one of the savvy ways people are earning an extra buck.

But if you aren't sure where to get your hands on one, read on to find out.

What are liquidation return pallets?

Liquidation return pallets are essentially boxes filled with assorted items that cannot be sold in a regular store.

There are a number of reasons why these items cannot be sold, such as damage.

Reselling items that cannot be sold in-store means that companies still generate some income from those products.

And consumers can get some of their favourite items at a fraction of the price.

John Lewis, Amazon, Argos and Very are a few of the popular UK retailers that sell liquidation pallets.

For you beauty enthusiasts out there, you can even get your hands on boxes filled with beauty supplies.

And if you're lucky you'll receive a pallet full of hidden gems that have no faults or minimal damage.

If they have either then you may be able to get them fixed.

Some people have been able to make a living out of refurbishing items from return pallets.

In December 2021, PaymentSense's annual report stated online returns alone cost UK retailers £5.2 billion a year.

The data showed "serial refunders" cost small businesses in the UK an average of £15,600 a month.

Furthermore, Analysis looked at the top 500 retailers in the UK to see which customers were looking the most for free returns.

Amazon took the top spot with an average of 4,400 searches per month with Asos, Shein, Zara, and Boohoo following closely behind.

Where can I buy a liquidation return pallet in the UK?

Buying a liquidation pallet isn't too difficult if you know where to look.

Also, what you find depends on what items and stores you are looking for as not all retailers provide this service.

Amazon's liquidation pallets can be bought directly through Amazon's auction programme.

Amazon Liquidation Auctions is the official marketplace where customers can buy surplus stock directly from Amazon.

In the UK, you can register on the Amazon Europe Liquidation Auctions marketplace separately.

All you need to do to bid on an auction is register with B-Stock and once you're approved, you're free to bid.

There is no fee required to apply or for being an approved buyer but you will need to be a valid reseller in order to apply.

Can you make money off liquidation pallets in the UK?

As TikTok has shown us, there are some very savvy shoppers who have managed to make decent money off these pallets.

Many have taken to social media to share their success stories and show how they made their money.

One couple was even able to make enough money to cover their mortgage payments by selling off their return pallet items.

Jamie and Sarah McCauley who live in West Michigan, got their hands on two boxes full of returned electrical equipment for £750.

Inside the pallets, the pair got lucky as they unwrapped four Nespresso machines, two microwaves, two deep fryers, and three Air Fryers.
