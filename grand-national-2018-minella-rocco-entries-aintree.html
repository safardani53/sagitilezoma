Grand National 2018 - Minella Rocco leads the way as 63 remain in hunt for Aintree glory

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Friday, June 14, 2024

JONJO O'NEILL's Minella Rocco remains at the head of the weights for Saturday's Randox Health Grand National at Aintree after the latest confirmation stage.

Last year's Gold Cup runner-up is one of 63 horses to stay in the hunt for glory in the famous race at the Merseyside course, although there were a few notable runners removed from the contest.

The Willie Mullins-trained trio of Bellshill, who ran so well in the Irish Grand National, Acapella Bourgeois and Rathvinden have been taken out at the five-day confirmation stage, as well as Minella Rocco's stablemate Go Conquer.

A maximum of 40 horses will line up in the four-and-a-quarter-mile feature, with Nigel Twiston-Davies' Double Ross currently occupying the final slot in the race.

Twiston-Davies also has leading hope Blaklion in the mix, while all the other major candidates including a strong Irish contingent were left in.

One who will be lining up for a third attempt is Vieux Lion Rouge, having finished seventh and sixth in the last two renewals.

"He usually lights up for the big occasion," trainer David Pipe told At The Races.

"He's run well in the last two Nationals and he won't mind what the ground is. It's just the first half of the season hasn't really gone to plan.
