You can now get GLITTER gravy to glam up your Christmas dinner... and here's how to make it at home

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Sunday, April 28, 2024

YOU can now get glitter gravy to add a touch of sparkle to your Christmas dinner - and it's sure to make that British classic look even more magical.

If you want to brighten your roast with the festive sauce this December, the good news is it can be made easily at home.

Edible glitter, or rainbow dust, can be bought from Tesco for £3.50 - or order it online with Amazon for £3.59.

Normally used in baking, the silver glitter can be added to any liquid to give it a shimmery glow.

The idea is the brainchild of the chefs at The Fox Under The Hill, a Hungry Horse pub on Shooters Hill Road in South East London, which is offering customers the chance to 'upgrade' their meals with the glitter gravy throughout December.

The boozer, which is known for its huge portions, will add the sauce to a selection of Christmas favourites, like traditional turkey with all trimmings, butternut squash and cashew nut roast, and the pub's Christmas pie.

It's currently only available in The Fox Under The Hill, and diners have to request it - or they'll be served up the bog standard brown stuff instead.

But the watering hole's general manager has hinted it could be rolled out across the country.

Ashish Patel told Metro.co.uk: "We’ve seen some wacky food trends over the years, and our glittery gravy is no exception – it’s the perfect way spread festive cheer in December.

"We’ve had some great reactions from guests so far as they add a bit of seasonal sparkle to their Christmas meals with our glittering gravy.

"It would be great to see the dish added to all Hungry Horse Christmas menus across the UK!"
