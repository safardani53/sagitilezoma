Blackpool B&amp;B boss responds to council's Viagra Hotel name ban by changing it... but it's just as ch

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Monday, May 20, 2024

A B&B boss who changed his hotel’s name to The Viagra has responded to council bosses who demanded he call it something else - but it's unlikely they'll like the new name either.

Neil Marshall, 47, sparked outrage when he rebranded The Vidella in Blackpool last month along with a cheeky sign that promised: "We'll keep you up all night!"

The local authority wrote to him requesting he change the name within 21 days - and he's done exactly what they asked.

A tourist spotted workers painting a new sign for the hotel earlier today, which now reads The Niagra Hotel - with the old 'V' crossed out.

The slogan "We'll keep you up all night!" plastered on the hotel's frontage has also been amended to "We'll keep you wet all night!"

Speaking to The Sun Online, Mr Marshall said: "They just told us to change the name - they didn't tell us to what.

"We are simply abiding by the council's demands.

"In this world there are some people who drink tea and coffee all day and don't have a sense of humour.

"There are others who drink lager and have a laugh - it's about opinions and people love it."

The defiant hotel boss claims customers - who pay £35 a pop to stay just a stone's throw away from Blackpool's world-famous promenade - love his fun approach.

Zak Laming, a tourist who spotted the new sign, said: "I've come over for the Micky Flanagan comedy gig and was staying at a hotel nearby - I think it's absolutely hilarious."

The 42-year-old, from Cheshire, added: "People are having a pop at the guy, but what's he supposed to do?

"There's lot's of competition in the area and he's just doing his bit to stand out. Good luck to him.

"It's also just typical Blackpool.

"It's a cheeky area and tourists find it funny and I think locals will too."

Clare Smith, president of Stay Blackpool, said the hotel should take care with its playful approach.

She said of its Viagra rebrand: "Blackpool is about something for everybody, coming and having a good time. The hotel is not in a family area so that's good, it's in the party area.

"There are over a 1,000 guests houses and B&Bs in Blackpool and you have to find your niche. This is being done very tongue in cheek. My concern would be they are opening themselves up to problems and putting off good quality guests."

Local councillor Ivan Taylor said: "I think it is inappropriate and in bad taste - we are a family resort and this leaves a bad impression."

Alan Cavill, director of Place at Blackpool Council, said: "Our trading standards team is also looking into it because it is also a brand name which is being used."
