3 facts about Monica Seles

  • Monica Seles started playing tennis with her brother with encouragement from her father. Who knew what started as a playful pastime would pave her pathway to fame.
  • When she competed in an American junior tournament, Seles was noticed by tennis coach Nick Bollettieri, who offered to train her.
  • Monica Seles struggled with binge eating for nearly a decade. The 41-year-old athlete attributed her struggle to deal with the pressures of sports, her father’s prostate cancer, and the stabbing incident that nearly ended her career in 1993. So, now she advocates raising awareness about the disorder.

Her significant achievements include

  • At 16 years and 6 months, Seles was the youngest French Open champion ever.
  • Seles was one of only two players to win the Australian Open on her first try.
  • She won the most consecutive matches at the Australian Open, i.e., 33.
  • Also, she became the only player to have won the Australian Open and French Open three times in a row.
  • At 16 years and 11 months, Monica is the youngest ever winner of the WTA year-end championships (1990)
  • Inducted into the 2009 International Tennis Hall of Fame and was called one of the “30 Legends of Women Tennis: Past, Present, and Future” by Time magazine.
  • She played in the 13 Grand Slam finals and won nine Grand Slam singles titles.
