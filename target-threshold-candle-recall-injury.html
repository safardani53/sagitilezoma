Target pulls popular candles off shelves after reports of cuts and burns

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Sunday, July 21, 2024

TARGET has recalled several scents of its Threshold candles.

The retailer pulled several jars of the popular candles after reports of cuts and burns.

People who own the candles are being told to stop using them immediately.

The Consumer Product Safety Commission said that the candle's jar can crack or break when in use.

The CPSC said Target received at least 137 reports of the jar breaking or cracking while the candle is lit.

Six injuries have been reported, including lacerations and severe burns.

RECALLED CANDLES

The recall affects nearly 5million Threshold candles.

The candles were sold at Target stores and target.com.

It includes the Threshold glass jar 5.5 ounce one-wick, 14 ounce three-wick, and 20 ounce three-wick candles in several scents.

Target said the recalled item numbers are printed on the bottom of the glass jars.

THRESHOLD CANDLE OWNERS

The candles were sold nationwide from August 2019 through March 2023.

The cost for the candles varied from $3 to $20.

If you own one of the affected candles, the CPSC said you should stop using it immediately.

You can return them to any Target store for a full refund.

You can also contact Target to receive a prepaid return label to return the candles by mail.

The U.S. Sun reached out to Target for comment.
