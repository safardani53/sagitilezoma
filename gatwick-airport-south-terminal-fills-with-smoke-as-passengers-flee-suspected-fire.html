Gatwick airport evacuated after fire alarm as south terminal fills with choking smoke

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Friday, August 16, 2024

GATWICK'S South terminal was evacuated amid a fire alert early today as passengers described corridors filled with choking smoke.

A fire alarm was triggered in the arrivals area of the Sussex airport around midnight.

Travellers were held on three jets as the terminal building was evacuated.

Airport bosses told The Sun Online they were investigating the cause of the fire alarm.

One passenger said on Twitter: "Just landed in Gatwick, walked through a long and really smoky corridor when we got off plane now Gatwick is being evacuated for a fire."

Shocked passengers say there was around a 30 minute delay between the corridor filling with smoke and the fire alarm sounding.

One passenger tweeted: "Raises concerns about security at Gatwick airport when it took an additional 30 minutes for the fire alarms to be activated after walking through a corridor filled with smoke..."

Another passenger told Sun Online: "[The alarm] went off 20 mins after I walked through the corridor and just as I was walking through, some people in fire vests and that were getting us to hurry up."

Other disgruntled passengers took to Twitter to share their shock at the delay between the alarm and a corridor filling with smoke.

Gatwick airport said: "We can confirm there was a fire alarm in the South Terminal and passengers were evacuated.

"We are investigating the cause of the alarm."

Gatwick's Twitter account said they were "conducting all the necessary checks after a fire alarm before allowing passengers to come through," in reply to one concerned passenger.

Later East Sussex Fire and Rescue Service said the alarm had ended and people were being allowed back into the building.
