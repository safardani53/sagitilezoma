Early Life, Family & Education

On December 9, 1987, Henderson was born to Gerald Henderson Sr. and Marie Henderson in Caldwell, New Jersey. He has a sibling, Jade Henderson.

Henderson’s father, Gerald, is also a former professional basketball player. His father played 13 seasons in NBA and won three championships. 

Growing up, Henderson was always surrounded by sports which may have ignited his spirit of playing basketball like his father. We can say that the playing heart is in the blood of the Henderson family. 

When Henderson was four years old, his father retired from sports. He always looked up to his father as every kid does while growing up. Hence he started playing basketball from a very young age.

Henderson was interested in golf while growing up and was a junior golfer from age 11 to 14.

He also played high jump and other sports in high school, but later, he decided to play basketball professionally. Gerald used to play golf with his father and had a great time playing matches.

Henderson grew up in a stable family as both his parents were economically sound, his father being a professional basketball player and his mother a real state agent.

Education

Henderson went to  Episcopal Academy in Merion, Pennsylvania. Gerald played basketball on the high school team.

After graduating from high school, he joined Duke University for his college studies. He was in college for three years and played college basketball.

Gerald Henderson: Career

Henderson was drafted into NBA with the 12th overall pick in 2009 by the Charlotte Bobcats. He signed a contract with the Bobcats on July 8, 2009.

In the 2011-2012 season, Henderson was able to showcase his excellent skills and performance for the team. He proved himself to be the leading player for the team in that season.

On March 12, 2013, Henderson scored career-high points in the match against the Boston Celtics and won. Seventeen days later, he scored the same points in the game against the New York Knicks. 

On June 28, 2013, Henderson got an offer from the Bobcats to be a restricted free agent. Just a month later, on July 30, 2013, Henderson was resigned from the Bobcats. 

Henderson recorded brilliantly with a season-high 31 points against the Toronto Raptors and led the fourth consecutive victory for his team on January 8, 2015.  

He was exchanged to the Portland Trail Blazers on June 24, 2015. 

On December 15, Henderson recorded 19-points, with career-high four three-point shots against the New Orleans Pelicans.

On July 9, 2016, Henderson entered a contract with the Philadelphia 76ers. After one year, on June 30, 2017, he was released from the contract by the 76ers.

Henderson retired from the NBA because of the injury, and his professional career as a basketball ended in 2017.

Gerald Henderson: Injuries & Retirement

Injuries are prevalent in the sports sector. Many players have to face such situations during the matches, practice matches, and some time off the court. Henderson also has a history of injuries in his professional basketball career.

Henderson has been open about his injuries and the treatment process. He told one news portal that he had six surgeries from 2008 to 2017 and had a history of many incidents in court.

The significant surgery that he had were wrist surgery, hip surgery, and achilles surgery.

Those surgeries piled up with other minor injuries, and it became challenging for him to continue as a basketball player. 

On August 2, 2017, Henderson had hip surgery, and he missed the whole 2017-2018 season. As it was a severe injury, it took a long time to recover, and it was his third hip surgery.

Henderson was planning to return in the 2018 off-season. Unfortunately, he suffered from an Achilles tendon during the practice match. 

Retirement

After the hip surgery, he had planned to try out one last time, but the tendon injury broke his spirit. The whole process of surgery and recovery was excruciating, both mentally and physically, so he had made up his mind by the time the injury happened.

Henderson joined the broadcast team of the Hornets in January 2019 as he somehow wanted to be around the game. But after such injuries and the surgeries, he finally thought of retiring and announced his retirement from NBA on April 30, 2019.

Henderson chose to leave rather than play with all the pain and anxiety. He had to retire at such a young age, but he believes that basketball is a part of his life, not his whole life, and we should appreciate his courage and willpower in making such a difficult decision.

Gerald Henderson: Charities

Henderson has been active in charity events and tournaments from his childhood days. He played in many celebrity charity tournaments. 

He has had a charity program running for a long time: the Gerald Henderson Charity Golf Invitational.

It benefits young men and empowers them in basketball, mentorship, and academics. The charity from the program supports Community Partnership School’s, Scholarship Fund.

In March 2012, he received the NBA’s Kia Community Assist Award for his outstanding efforts in the community and constant philanthropic and charitable work.

Gerald Henderson: Net Worth

Henderson’s net worth is around $16 million. The 76ers signed him with an annual salary of $8.802 million in 2016. His earnings include his deals with different clubs as a professional basketball player. 

Besides being a professional basketball player, Henderson also has other sources of income. He is a co-owner of a company called Active Dreamers.

The company is an NBA-licensed bedding and pillow company. As he is the co-owner indeed, he gets a share of the enormous profit of the company.

Gerald Henderson: Wife & Kids

Henderson is married to Nilou Henderson, a real estate agent. The couple has two daughters, Roya and Zara, together.

The couple was in a two-year relationship before finally tying the knot in 2019. Henderson often shares pictures of his wife and kids on his social media handles, showing his love and affection for them.

After retiring from NBA, Henderson has been spending a lot of time with his family. He is back to his duties as a father, which is missed in the hectic schedules during his professional career. It shows that he is a family man and respects values and love.
