George Chelston Springer III is an American professional baseball player for the Toronto Major League Baseball Blue Jays baseball team. He played for the Houston Astros from 2014-2020. Astros picked Springer in the first round of the 2011 MLB Draft. He made his MLB debut in 2014, for the Astros. Springer played mostly on the right and also spent a lot of time in the middle. Originally from New Britain, Connecticut, Springer is of Puerto Rican and Panamanian descent. Springer played varsity baseball at the University of Connecticut, where he was named the Big East Conference Baseball Player of the Year and U.S. First Team. In 2017, Springer became the MLB All-Star, Silver Slugger Award and World Series Champion champion. He was also named the 2017 World Series MVP, clocking a record five home runs as Astros beat the Los Angeles Dodgers in seven games. He is All Star again in 2018 and 2019 .
