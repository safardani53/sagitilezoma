Female pedestrian, 70, dies after car crash in Manchester as man is arrested for dangerous driving

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Monday, June 3, 2024

A PENSIONER has died after a fatal collision in Manchester and a man has been arrested in connection with her death. 

The 70-year-old woman was hit by a car on Princess Road, in Hulme, at around 12.50am on Thursday. 

Police believe that two cars crashed after one of them swerved and changed lanes, with the second car hitting a lamppost. 

Officers have arrested a 34-year-old man, who was driving one of the cars, on suspicion of causing death by dangerous driving. 

The woman, who was a pedestrian, was pronounced dead at the scene and a cordon remains in place. 

Princess Road has been closed off from Claremont Road to Great Western Street while GMP's Serious Collision Investigation Unit carry out investigation work.

Police Sergeant Andrew Page, of GMPs Serious Collision Investigation Unit said: "This is a tragic incident in which a woman has very sadly lost her life and our thoughts are with her loved ones at this incredibly difficult time.
