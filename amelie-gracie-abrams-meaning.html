What Is the Meaning of Gracie Abrams' Song &quot;Amelie&quot;?

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Wednesday, January 31, 2024

What is the meaning of "Amelie" by Gracie Abrams? Unpacking the new offering from the rising musical sensation.

One thing is for certain: it's a good time to be a Gracie Abrams fan. The budding singer/songwriter has been slowly rolling out her latest album, "Good Riddance," to positive critical and fan reception. On Feb. 10, 2023, she decided to drop one of the leading songs off the album ahead of its full release.

Titled "Amelie," the soft acoustic ballad is already resonating with millions of listeners worldwide, but what does it mean? Keep reading for a lyrical breakdown of "Amelie."

On "Amelie," Gracie Abrams gives meaning to short, yet impactful relationships.

Longstanding, deeply-rooted emotions are not of Gracie's concern on "Amelie." Instead, the Gen-Z darling decides to romanticize fleeting romance. This is exemplified almost immediately when she leads the song off with the lyrics, "I met a girl once / She sorta ripped me open / She doesn't even know it / She doesn't know my name."

Article continues below advertisement

Immediately after that, Gracie dives right into the song's chorus singing words of longing for Amelie as well as questioning if the entire interaction was real or a figment of her imagination. She sings, "Where did you go / Amelie, Amelie, Amelie? / Where'd you go? / Or were you all in a dream / Amelie, Amelie? / I don't know."

Gracie then recalls in the following verse that, "She had her hair up / She cried about her obsessions / But she doesn't know I'd let her / Ruin all my days."

After reprising the chorus, Gracie makes it known that despite the questions and apprehensions she is facing, she's wholeheartedly Amelie's. "Tell me more, I would give all my time / All your words felt like a nursery rhyme / Comfortable, handin' you my whole life / When all of your words felt like a funeral rite," she sings.

To close out the moving track, Gracie looks back to the chorus once more, repeating the phrase, "Where did you go / Amelie, Amelie, Amelie? / Where'd you go? / Or were you all in a dream," as if in some type of affirmation, reminding her that the fleeting love she longs for is now gone.

Be sure to check out the rest of Gracie's new album "Good Riddance" on all major streaming services.
