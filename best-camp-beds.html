Best Camp Beds 2019 | The Sun UK

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Monday, July 29, 2024

OUR roundup of the best camp beds means you don’t have to forego a good night’s sleep when you’re away from home, or have your guests do the same.

Gone are the days when you’d lay on what felt like the world’s thinnest yoga mat and wonder why you woke up the next morning feeling like you’d broken your back.

Now there are plenty of options to ensure you have a restful night - and they can also double up as an emergency bed for those times when you have more guests than places to sleep.

Here's a quick run-down of your choices:

Inflatable beds

These are filled with air – usually via an electric pump – and placed directly on the floor or groundsheet in a tent and act as a mattress.

Because they’re inflatable, then can be filled with the maximum amount of air for a firm feel, or a little less if you prefer a softer mattress. Once the air has been let out, they fold down neatly to a compact size for easy, lightweight transportation.

Some also have integrated pillows, meaning you have one less thing to pack.

Best for: Adjustable comfort that’s compact when packed away.

Fold-away beds

This style has a lightweight metal frame with a canvas sheet stretched over and fixed to the frame, much like a stretcher. Some are topped with a thin foam mattress. They’re on legs so sit away from the ground, making them a warmer option than ground-laying inflatable beds. They come in single or double sizes and are super sturdy, but the firmness cannot be adjusted. They fold down when not in use and do require a fair amount of storage space.

Best for: Keeping ground chills at bay, and for those who toss and turn at night and need a sturdy bed.

Bunk beds

Believe it or not, you can get bunk bed-style camp beds - they’re the same as the fold-away design with a metal frame and canvas sheets, just double the size. They can be positioned on top of each other or side-by-side, so they’re extremely versatile.

Best for: Families and large groups.
