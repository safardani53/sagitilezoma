Clu Gulager dead at 93: Cowboy movie star who appeared alongside Ronald Reagan in The Killers passes

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Tuesday, September 24, 2024

CLU Gulager, a cowboy movie star who appeared alongside Ronald Reagan in The Killers, has died at age 93.

The actor, who was a real-life cowboy from Oklahoma, was known for his legendary roles on The Last Picture Show, The Tall Man, and The Virginian.

Gulager, who's first name is actually William, passed away from natural causes at the Los Angeles home of his son, John, and his daughter-in-law, Diane, on Friday, they told The Hollywood Reporter.

“Clu was as caring as he was loyal and devoted to his craft, a proud member of the Cherokee nation, a rule-breaker, sharp and astute and on the side — always — of the oppressed,” his family said.

“He was good-humored, an avid reader, tender and kind. Loud and dangerous," they said.

His most recent films include: Tangerine in 2015, Blue Jay in 2016, and Once Upon a Time in Hollywood in 2019.

The tv star also appeared in horror films such as The Return of the Living Dead and A Nightmare on Elm Street Part 2: Freddy's Revenge, both of which premiered in 1985.

In 2019, Gulager recalled what it was like to be a cowboy turned actor.

“And then later on, you move on and something comes over you, and you want to be an actor. Well, I could play a cowboy, and it was easy for me to ride a pony and wear a hat,” he said.

Back in 1964, Gulager played Charlie Strom, the ultimate hitman, with Ronald Reagan who played a mob boss in The Killers.

“I was surprised that Lee Marvin let me do all that stuff, actually,” Gulager said in a 2020 interview.

“But the director wanted me to invent some things to [make the character] a psychotic, really a nut. So I tried to go along with that," he said.

The star's performance in The Killers landed him the role of the caddish oil-field foreman in The Last Picture Show.

In 1960, Gulager played the iconic Billy the Kid with Barry Sullivan as Pat Garett in The Tall Man.

The series ended in two short seasons, partly because Congress didn't like how Billy, a criminal, was being "inaccurately" portrayed as a hero to younger viewers, according to The Hollywood Reporter.

However, Gulager pointed out in a 2015 interview that The Untouchables was very violent and still allowed to be aired.

“I played a character on that called ‘Mad Dog’ Coll where I shot a horse in a horse race, killed a little boy in Brooklyn and cut off a bartender’s fingers," the star said.

"But they left that on because they thought that show was historically accurate," said Gulager.

The Hollywood icon also appeared on more than 50 episodes of The Virginian from 1964 to 1968.

He played Deputy Sheriff Emmett Ryker alongside tv stars James Drury and Doug McClure.

“I was broke when I stepped onto [The Virginian],” he said in a 2014 interview.

“I had to ask Frank Price, who ran it, for a job. He fired an actor off the set and hired me. Had I known he had fired someone, I would not have accepted the job,” Gulager said.

Some of Gulager's other roles included a race car mechanic in Winning with Paul Newman and a detective in McQ with John Wayne.

Gulager also worked on films that his son, John, directed such as the horror film Feast in 2005 and its two sequels and Piranha 3DD in 2012.

The star was married to singer-actress Miriam Byrd-Nethery from 1952 to 2003, after she had passed away.
