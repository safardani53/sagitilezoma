Who is Big Zuu and what is his net worth?

Posted by Martina Birk on Monday, June 17, 2024

BIG Zuu is best know for being a rapper, performing at some of the UK's most popular festivals including Boomtown and Lovebox.

But his talent doesn't stop there, the star is also the host of a popular food show on Dave.

Who is Big Zuu?

Zuhair Hassan professionally known as Big Zuu, is a British rapper, grime MC, songwriter, DJ and TV personality from West London.

He was born on August 19, 1995.

The rapper spent two years at Goldsmiths University studying towards a community development and youth work degree.

He is a member of the MTP crew, which consists of his cousin AJ Tracey and other grime artists.

In a interview with Red Bull in 2018, Hassan described that his music style was influenced by "old school" artists such as Tupac Shakur, Kme, P Money, Ghetts and Skepta.

What TV shows has Big Zuu been on?

In 2019, the UKTV channel Dave commissioned a new food show, Big Zuu's Big Eats, which starred Big Zuu and a number of stand-up comedians.

The show was a ten-part series, and saw Big Zuu and his two friends, Tubsey and Hyder, all cook for different comedians while touring the UK.

His comedian guests on the show included: Ed Gamble, Jamali Maddix, Jimmy Carr, Josh Widdicombe and London Hughes to name a few.

Aside from this, Big Zuu has appeared on season two of the game show Guessable, and Jon & Lucy's Christmas Sleepover.

He also presented Big Zuu's Breakfast Show on Sunday mornings throughout the summer 2022 on ITV.

Is Big Zuu married?

By the looks of it, Big Zuu is single.

However, the star could just like to keep that side of his life private.

What is Big Zuu's net worth?

Idolnetworth estimates Big Zuu’s net worth to around £2.5m.

The rapper has performed at festivals such as Lovebox, Boomtown and at the O2 Academy Islington in London.

He has more than 34,000 followers on his YouTube channel where he can be found here.

His most popular songs include Magical, The Advert, Variation, Duppy and No Limits.
